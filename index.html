Learnings & Findings · East Meets Feed
What We Learned
One authentic voice, the right system,
three platforms — and the numbers follow.
Strategy Learnings
Analytical Insights
S1 Platform differentiation is not optional Strategy
IG builds brand identity through aesthetic polish. Douyin drives mass follower growth through algorithm and emotional hooks. XHS activates niche community trust. Treating them as one channel wastes each platform's native advantage.
3 platforms · 3 content strategies · 1 brand voice
S2 The workflow is a loop, not a funnel Process
Research → Brief → Shoot → Adapt → Post → Measure → Optimize. One NYC outing feeds all three platforms, and every engagement metric teaches the next brief. The system compounds with each cycle.
7-step loop · 3–5 posts per outing · efficiency compounds
S3 Personas are hypotheses, not blueprints Audience
We planned for Mina (IG-first aesthete) and Lummy (Douyin-native). The algorithm surfaced Male 24–30 as Douyin's primary demographic — a segment never in the original brief. Personas should guide content, but the data should revise them.
Planned audience ≠ reached audience · algorithm reveals truth
S4 Cultural translation > content localization Content
The strongest Douyin posts weren't translated from English — they were conceived in Chinese emotional logic: introspection, solitude in the city, quiet yearning. Authentic cultural framing consistently outperformed localized captions.
Emotional frame in Chinese → highest follower conversion
A1 Cover CTR is the algorithm's gate ×16 lift
Night mood covers sent CTR from 4% → 66.67% — a single variable shift. High CTR signals Douyin to push the post to the next audience wave. Cover is not aesthetics; it's distribution infrastructure.
4% → 66.67% · Night > Daytime > Carousel
A2 89% of reach was algorithm-driven Organic

Learnings & Findings
What We Learned
One authentic voice, the right system, three platforms — and the numbers follow.
Strategy Learnings
S1Platform differentiation is not optional
IG = brand identity. Douyin = mass growth. XHS = community trust. Each needs its own native content logic — not repurposed versions.
3 platforms · 3 strategies · 1 brand voice
S2The workflow is a loop, not a funnel
Research → Brief → Shoot → Adapt → Post → Measure → Optimize. One NYC outing feeds all three platforms. Every metric teaches the next brief.
7-step loop · efficiency compounds with each cycle
S3Personas are hypotheses, not blueprints
We planned for Mina and Lummy. The algorithm surfaced Male 24–30 as Douyin's primary segment — unplanned. Data should revise personas, not the other way around.
Planned audience ≠ reached audience · algorithm reveals truth
S4Cultural translation beats localization
Best Douyin posts were conceived in Chinese emotional logic — introspection, solitude, quiet yearning — not translated from English. Authentic framing beats localized captions.
Chinese emotional frame → highest follower conversion
Analytical Insights
A1Cover CTR is the algorithm's distribution gate
Night mood covers sent CTR 4% → 66.67% (×16). High CTR signals Douyin to push the post wider. Cover is distribution infrastructure, not aesthetics.

89% recommended · organic ≠ existing network

9 posts · 139K total views · 11.9K avg/post. Top post alone (33K) = 24% of total campaign views. Consistency builds the trust that keeps content surfacing.
